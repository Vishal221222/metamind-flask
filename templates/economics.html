Behavioral Economics

Behavioral economics studies how emotions, habits, biases, and social influence affect financial decisions, markets, and everyday choices.

Start Learning

What is Behavioral Economics?

Behavioral economics combines psychology and economics. It explains why people do not always make perfectly rational decisions. Instead, people use shortcuts, emotions, past experiences, and social signals while making choices.

Loss Aversion

People feel losses more strongly than equal gains.

Anchoring Bias

The first information we see affects our final decision.

Nudge Theory

Small design changes can guide people toward better decisions.

Quick Learning Guide

How to Study Behavioral Economics

Start with basic human biases, then learn how these biases affect money, marketing, business, politics, and daily decisions.

Step 1: Learn core biases like loss aversion, anchoring, and framing.

Step 2: Understand real-life examples from shopping, saving, and investing.

Step 3: Study nudge theory and how choices are designed.

Step 4: Apply concepts to ads, business, apps, and public policy.
