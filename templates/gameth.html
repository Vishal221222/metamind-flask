Game Theory

Game theory is the study of strategic decision-making where outcomes depend on the actions of multiple players.

Start Learning

What is Game Theory?

Game theory is a mathematical and economic framework used to analyze situations where individuals or groups make decisions that affect each other. It is widely used in economics, business, politics, psychology, and AI.

Players

Decision makers in the game (individuals, firms, governments).

Strategies

Possible actions each player can take.

Payoffs

The outcome or reward each player receives.

Key Concepts

Nash Equilibrium

A situation where no player can benefit by changing their strategy alone.

Dominant Strategy

The best strategy regardless of what others do.

Zero-Sum Game

One player's gain is another player's loss.

Prisoner’s Dilemma

Shows why rational individuals may not cooperate.

Mixed Strategy

Players randomize actions to remain unpredictable.

Repeated Games

Games played multiple times influencing long-term strategy.

Real Life Applications

Business

Pricing strategies, competition, advertising decisions.

Politics

Voting systems, negotiations, international relations.

Technology

AI strategy, cybersecurity, algorithmic decisions.

Everyday Life

Negotiations, relationships, decision-making.
